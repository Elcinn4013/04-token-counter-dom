What is an LLM?

LLM stands for Large Language Model. It is a highly advanced type of artificial intelligence designed to understand, generate, and interact with human language.

LLMs work by being trained on massive datasets containing billions of words from books, articles, and websites. Through deep learning neural networks, they learn the complex statistical relationships between words, allowing them to accurately predict the next most likely token in a sequence to generate coherent text.

Some popular real-world examples of LLMs include GPT-4o by OpenAI and Claude 3.5 Sonnet by Anthropic.

What is a Token?

In the context of LLMs, a token is the fundamental unit of data processed by the AI. A token is not always a full word; it is simply a chunk of text. On average, in English, a single token is roughly 3-4 characters long, or about ¾ of a typical word.

Tokens matter deeply because they are the primary unit of measurement in AI. A model's context window is measured in tokens (defining how much text it can process at once), and providers base their pricing strictly on the number of tokens processed.

For example, the simple sentence "Hello, world!" might be split into four distinct tokens: ["Hello", ",", " world", "!"]

English vs. Azerbaijani: AI models are primarily trained on English text, so tokenization is highly optimized for it. A standard English word is usually 1 token. However, in Azerbaijani, a word like "gələcəyik" might be split into 3 or 4 separate tokens because the model doesn't recognize it as a single unit. As a result, processing Azerbaijani text consumes significantly more tokens and costs more compared to English.
